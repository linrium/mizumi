# Cross-Company Journey Dashboard: Business Guide

This dashboard explains how VietJet Air and HDBank can create more value
together. It is written for business teams who need to answer practical
questions:

- Where is the biggest partnership opportunity?
- Which customers should we focus on first?
- What offer should we send them?
- Which channel should we use?
- How do we protect revenue from customers who had a bad experience?

The dashboard is useful because it connects customer behavior from both
companies into one business story. VietJet knows travel behavior. HDBank knows
banking behavior. When those signals are combined, both companies can find
customers who are more likely to buy, activate, upgrade, or stay loyal.

## The Business Problem

VietJet and HDBank both have valuable customer relationships, but the value is
not fully shared yet.

HDBank may have customers who spend on travel but have not built a VietJet
relationship. VietJet may have frequent flyers or high-value travel customers
who do not yet use HDBank products. Both sides can see part of the customer
journey, but neither company has the full picture alone.

Without this dashboard, the partnership can easily become too broad:

- Send the same campaign to too many customers.
- Focus on customer count instead of customer value.
- Miss high-value customers who are ready to activate.
- Ignore customers who need service recovery before they can be cross-sold.
- Run campaigns without knowing which offer or channel should be used.

The dashboard solves this by showing where the value is, why it exists, and
what action should happen next.

## How to Read This Dashboard

Think of the dashboard as a business story that moves from opportunity to
action.

It starts by showing where VietJet and HDBank can create value for each other.
Then it shows which valuable customers have not been activated yet. After
that, it helps the teams choose the right customers, the right offer, and the
right channel.

In simple terms, the dashboard helps answer five business questions:

1. Where is the biggest partnership value?
2. Which customers are still untapped?
3. Which customer groups should we act on first?
4. What offer or recovery action should they receive?
5. Which channel should deliver it?

So the dashboard is not just a report. It is a decision tool. It helps the
business move from "there is an opportunity" to "this is the next campaign we
should run."

## Cross-Company Opportunity Generated

This panel answers the first leadership question: which company is creating
more business opportunity for the other?

If VietJet customers show strong potential for HDBank products, the dashboard
shows that as value from VietJet to HDBank. If HDBank customers show strong
potential for VietJet travel offers, it shows that as value from HDBank to
VietJet.

Why this matters:

- HDBank can see how much banking opportunity comes from VietJet customers.
- VietJet can see how much travel opportunity comes from HDBank customers.
- Both companies can understand which side of the partnership should receive
  more investment first.

The value is not just the number of customers. It is the business value of the
opportunity.

## Untapped Cross-Sell Whitespace

This panel shows the customers who already look valuable but have not been
activated by the partner company yet.

For example:

- An HDBank customer already spends money on travel but has no VietJet
  relationship.
- A VietJet customer already books flights or travel but has no HDBank
  relationship.

This is the "white space" in the partnership. These customers are not cold
leads. They already show behavior that suggests a partner offer could make
sense.

Why this matters:

- Campaigns can focus on customers who already have demand.
- The business avoids wasting budget on low-intent audiences.
- Each company can find the partner's best open opportunity pool.

This panel helps the teams decide where the easiest and largest growth
opportunity may be.

## Journey Edge Value

This panel shows how value moves through the partnership journey.

Instead of only saying "we have customers," it shows which customer situations
create value. For example, one group may be valuable because they are frequent
flyers without a banking relationship. Another group may be valuable because
they are bank customers with strong travel spending.

Why this matters:

- Business teams can see which customer journeys are worth acting on.
- The partnership can focus on the journeys that create the most value.
- Smaller journeys can still be reviewed if they are strategic or high margin.

This panel helps both companies understand the "why" behind the opportunity,
not only the size of the opportunity.

## Journey Funnel: HDBank Travel Customers to VietJet Activation

This panel turns the opportunity into a practical activation funnel.

It starts with all HDBank customers, then narrows down to customers who spend
on travel, then customers who spend on airline or online travel services, then
customers who do not yet have a VietJet relationship, and finally customers
who are ready for activation.

Why this matters:

- HDBank can see how many of its customers are realistic VietJet prospects.
- VietJet can understand the quality of the audience coming from HDBank.
- Both companies can see where the audience becomes smaller and why.

This avoids a common campaign mistake: assuming the full customer base is the
real opportunity. The real opportunity is the part of the customer base that
has clear travel behavior and is ready to act.

## Activation Segments

This panel groups customers into simple business segments so teams know how to
approach them.

The main idea is:

- Some customers are ready now.
- Some should be led by HDBank.
- Some should be led by VietJet.
- Some need more nurturing before a strong sales push.

Why this matters:

- High-readiness customers can receive stronger offers sooner.
- HDBank-led customers can be approached through banking trust and financial
  products.
- VietJet-led customers can be approached through travel benefits and flight
  behavior.
- Nurture customers can receive softer messages instead of aggressive sales.

This helps the partnership avoid one-size-fits-all campaigns. Each customer
group gets a more suitable message.

## Recovery Risk to Revenue

This panel focuses on customers who may be valuable but had a poor experience,
such as incidents or delays.

These customers matter because they may still have high future value, but they
may not respond well to a normal cross-sell offer. They may need recovery,
care, or reassurance first.

Why this matters:

- VietJet can protect high-value customers after service issues.
- HDBank can avoid sending the wrong offer at the wrong time.
- Both companies can reduce churn risk before pushing new products.

This panel changes the question from "who can we sell to?" to "who do we need
to recover before we sell?"

That creates better customer experience and protects future revenue.

## Next Best Journey

This panel shows what should happen next for each customer journey.

It connects the customer situation to a recommended offer and then to a
recommended channel, such as app, email, SMS, or relationship manager.

Why this matters:

- Teams can move from analysis to action.
- Campaign owners can see which offer should go to which audience.
- Channel teams can understand where demand will land.
- Relationship managers can focus on higher-value or more complex customers.

This is where the dashboard becomes an operating tool. It does not only
describe the customer; it helps choose the next move.

## Offer Routing

This panel shows the final path from business signal to campaign execution.

It answers: where did the opportunity come from, what use case does it belong
to, and which channel should handle it?

Why this matters:

- Leaders can see whether the biggest opportunities are actually routed to
  action.
- Campaign teams can check whether the right channels are being used.
- Operations teams can see if one channel is overloaded.
- The partnership can measure whether value is moving from insight to
  execution.

This panel closes the loop. It makes sure the partnership does not stop at
finding opportunities, but actually sends the right offer through the right
path.

## How It Creates Shared Partnership Value

The biggest value comes from the shared view. Each company has useful customer
signals, but the combined picture is more powerful.

The dashboard helps the partnership:

- Agree on the size of the opportunity.
- Decide which direction of the partnership to prioritize.
- Choose the best customer groups for activation.
- Reduce poor campaign targeting.
- Improve customer experience by matching offer, timing, and channel.
- Track whether opportunities are actually becoming actions.

In simple terms, the dashboard helps VietJet and HDBank move from "we should
partner more" to "these are the customers, this is the value, this is the
offer, this is the channel, and this is the next action."
